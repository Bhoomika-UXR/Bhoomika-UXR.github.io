Clarity, Role Definition & Insight Flow: Research to Scale a High-Frequency Design Review Program

Led research across a large-scale design review program to address growing inconsistency and ambiguity as participation scaled across teams - identifying gaps that reduced review effectiveness and limited how learnings informed design system improvements.

Role

UX Research Lead

Scope

End-to-end program research

Sessions Observed

12 live review sessions

Methods

Stakeholder interviews · Contextual inquiry · Focus groups · Semi-structured interviews

↗

Program Consistency

Standardized expectations, roles, and session structure across a high-frequency review program running 4-6 sessions daily - improving consistency and reducing repeated clarification at scale.

Cross-Functional Alignment

Background

Context & Problem Space

A structured design review program was used across the organization to ensure product experiences met a high bar for usability, accessibility, and consistency prior to launch. The program was a required review for new services and high-priority feature launches - making it a critical quality gate before release.

Sessions ran at high frequency - 4 to 6 per day - with each session lasting one hour and involving cross-functional participants including reviewers, facilitators, design system ambassadors, and product teams. As adoption increased and the program scaled across multiple teams, differences in expectations and inconsistent feedback practices created friction.

Scaling introduced risk across three dimensions:

  • - Review effectiveness and consistency across teams
  • - Role clarity across reviewers, facilitators, and ambassadors
  • - The ability to translate recurring insights into design system improvements
Process

Research Approach

A multi-method study designed to understand the design review experience across the full ecosystem - capturing perspectives from all roles involved.

01

Stakeholder Interviews

Problem definition

Interviews with program and design system leadership to define the problem space, surface competing priorities, and establish learning goals before fieldwork began.

Output Defined research focus areas and surfaced where leadership perceived the program was falling short
02

Contextual Inquiry - Live Session Observation

12 sessions observed

Observed 12 live design review sessions to capture friction, role confusion, and inconsistent feedback practices as they occurred in practice - rather than relying on participant recall alone.

Focus areas Session structure - role behavior - feedback consistency - insight capture practices
Why contextual inquiry At 4-6 sessions per day, observed behavior revealed patterns that interviews alone would not have surfaced
03

Focus Groups with Product Teams

Participant perspective

Focus groups with product teams participating in reviews to understand their experience of the process - including how they prepared, how they interpreted feedback, and where the review felt unclear or inconsistent.

Key contribution Surfaced the participant-side experience of role ambiguity - distinct from how reviewers and facilitators perceived the same sessions
04

Semi-Structured Interviews with Reviewers, Facilitators & Ambassadors

Role-specific depth

In-depth interviews with reviewers, facilitators, and design system ambassadors to understand how each role experienced the program - including where responsibilities overlapped, where expectations were unclear, and where insights were lost after sessions ended.

Output Role-specific findings that enabled targeted recommendations for each participant type rather than generic program improvements
Research Impact

From Insight to Program Improvement

How three research insights translated into concrete program improvements across a high-frequency enterprise review process.

Findings

Key Insights

Three findings that together diagnosed why a high-frequency review program was losing consistency and impact as it scaled.

Primary findings - program-driving

01 · Expectations

Unclear Expectations Reduced Session Effectiveness

When goals, success criteria, and how feedback would be used were not established early, participants were less confident in how to engage and how to apply feedback after sessions ended.

Improvement influenced

Centralized onboarding wiki clarifying goals, roles, and session structure

02 · Role Clarity

Ambiguity Around Roles Created Inefficiency

Participants were often unsure how responsibilities differed among reviewers, ambassadors, and facilitators - leading to hesitation, duplicated effort, and inconsistent engagement across sessions.

Improvement influenced

Reusable session structures with defined responsibilities per role

03 · Insight Flow

Insight Flow Was Inconsistent Across Sessions

Recurring themes surfaced across reviews, but there was no structured way to capture or route insights to inform design system guidance and prioritization. Valuable patterns were identified but not systematically shared.

Improvement influenced

Structured feedback loop connecting reviewers and ambassadors to the design system team

Outcomes

Impact & Decisions Influenced

verified

Improved effectiveness of a critical product launch gate

The design review program was a required step for new services and high-priority feature launches. Improving clarity and consistency increased the effectiveness of reviews as a quality gate, enabling teams to engage more confidently and apply feedback more reliably before launch.

tune

Improved consistency across high-frequency sessions

With 4-6 one-hour sessions conducted daily, standardizing expectations, roles, and session structure reduced repeated clarification and improved consistency across reviews at scale.

group

Reduced coordination overhead across cross-functional participants

Clarifying roles and introducing reusable session structures reduced ambiguity around responsibilities, enabling participants to engage more efficiently and reducing hesitation during reviews.

school

Reduced onboarding time and repeated clarification

A centralized onboarding wiki clarified roles, expectations, and session structure - reducing reliance on repeated explanations and enabling participants to engage more effectively from the start.

hub

Enabled structured feedback loop to inform design system improvements

Established a mechanism for reviewers and ambassadors to share recurring insights with the design system team - improving visibility into common patterns and informing system-level guidance.

handshake

Aligned stakeholders across program and design system leadership

Delivered readouts across leadership groups to align on key challenges and recommendations - supporting adoption of improvements and future program evolution.

Researcher Reflection
"Program experiences scale successfully when expectations are explicit, roles are clearly defined, and insights flow efficiently between participants and platform teams."

Improving clarity, strengthening onboarding, and enabling structured feedback loops not only increased consistency across sessions - it also strengthened how design reviews contributed to broader system improvements.

This work demonstrated that operational research - studying how programs run, not just what products do - is a meaningful lever for improving quality and consistency at enterprise scale.
