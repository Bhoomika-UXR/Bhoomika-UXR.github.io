Background

Context & Problem Space

As adoption of a large enterprise design system expanded across the platform, understanding builder needs at scale became essential. Systemic friction points were going undetected between research cycles, and there was no structured mechanism to track whether recommendations were working once implemented.

I led a continuous feedback initiative combining biannual CSAT surveys with facilitated listening sessions - creating a research program designed not just to surface insights, but to measure their impact over time.

The program needed to answer three questions continuously:

  • -What friction are builder teams experiencing right now?
  • -Are the improvements we made based on previous research actually working?
  • -What should the design system prioritize next?
Process

Research Approach

A continuous program combining large-scale feedback collection with facilitated deep-dive sessions - designed to surface trends while preserving contextual depth.

01

Survey Redesign & Statistical Analysis

~300 responses per cycle

Redesigned biannual CSAT surveys to improve clarity, accessibility, and relevance. Applied statistical analysis to identify trends across cycles and proactively added baseline metrics to track the downstream impact of specific recommendations over time.

Key design decision Added a new metric in Q3 2023 specifically to baseline builder awareness of releases - enabling a before/after comparison once communication improvements were implemented
02

Thematic Analysis of Open-Text Feedback

Cross-cycle synthesis

Conducted thematic analysis of open-text responses across survey cycles to surface recurring patterns that quantitative scores alone would not reveal - identifying specific friction points including content consistency gaps, documentation usability issues, and contribution process ambiguity.

Example finding Only 61% of respondents were aware of the exact contributions process - directly validating and accelerating a major contributions revamp
03

Facilitated Listening Sessions

Service team depth

Facilitated listening sessions with major service teams structured around pre-collected discussion topics - surfacing workflow challenges, decision-making friction, and collaboration needs that surveys could not fully capture.

Key contribution Action tracking sessions with squad leads led directly to the inception of multiple organizational initiatives and goals
04

Cross-Functional Workshops & Stakeholder Readouts

Strategic alignment

Organized cross-functional workshops and readouts to translate findings into prioritized improvements - aligning stakeholders on priorities and enabling teams to take ownership of specific recommendations.

Output Findings shaped organizational priorities adopted across outreach, engineering, product, and design functional teams
Research Impact

From Insight to Organizational Initiative

Four research findings that directly launched organizational initiatives - each tracked over time to measure whether the recommendation worked.

Findings

Key Insights

Four findings that surfaced consistently across survey cycles and listening sessions - each driving a distinct organizational response.

Primary findings - initiative-driving

01 - Transparency

Builders Needed Rationale Behind Guidance

Builder teams needed clearer explanations of the reasoning behind component guidance to support stakeholder alignment and reduce pushback during implementation. Without this context, builders struggled to advocate for design system standards internally.

Initiative launched

Research visibility program - 2024 org goal - 1,500+ visits to date

02 - Updates Awareness

Builders Shared Difficulty Staying Informed

Builders struggled to stay informed about updates and priorities. This finding was tracked longitudinally - a baseline metric added in Q3 2023 showed a 6 percentage point improvement by Q1 2024 after communication changes were implemented.

Initiative launched

Releases and Roadmap Share Sessions - 60+ attendees at first session

03 - Contribution Pathways

Teams Wanted to Contribute but Lacked Clear Pathways

Teams showed willingness to contribute reusable solutions but needed clearer processes and guidance pathways. Only 61% of respondents were aware of the exact contributions process - directly validating and accelerating a major contributions revamp.

Initiative launched

New contributions article clarifying effort expectations and process steps

Additional finding - deepened the picture

04 - Documentation

Implementation Friction Driven by Documentation Usability

Friction was driven more by how guidance was presented than by the complexity of the components themselves. Builders relied on clear documentation structure and practical examples to implement solutions more efficiently.

Researcher Reflection
"Continuous feedback is only valuable if you close the loop. Research earns trust not by finding problems, but by measuring whether the solutions worked."

By improving how feedback was gathered and acted upon, this program strengthened transparency, trust, and shared ownership - ensuring the design system evolved in alignment with builder needs rather than internal assumptions.

The most important methodological decision was adding a baseline metric in Q3 2023 specifically to track the impact of a recommendation not yet implemented. That single design choice transformed a feedback program into a longitudinal study of organizational change.
