Background

Context & Problem Space

As AI-powered functionality expanded across a large enterprise platform, builder teams began implementing diverse interaction approaches with limited shared guidance. Without consistent patterns, teams were independently solving the same problems — creating fragmentation in how AI behavior was communicated to users.

Simultaneously, the design system needed to evolve to support emerging AI interaction models while enabling teams to contribute solutions back into the shared ecosystem. Without foundational research, design system investment risked being misaligned with real builder needs.

Fragmentation created risk across three dimensions:

  • — Consistency of AI interactions across products
  • — Clear communication of AI behavior and trust signals to users
  • — Reuse of patterns and components across teams — limiting ecosystem scalability
Process

Research Approach

Multi-method foundational research designed to surface both strategic priorities and ground-level builder workflows.

01

Stakeholder Interviews

Strategic alignment

Interviews with design system leadership and cross-functional stakeholders to surface open questions, competing priorities, and where the design system was perceived to be falling short in supporting AI experiences.

Output Defined research focus areas and surfaced gaps in existing design system strategy before builder interviews began
02

Builder Team Interviews & Workflow Mapping

Qualitative inquiry

In-depth qualitative interviews with builder teams (designers, developers, PMs) to understand how they were implementing AI interactions in practice — including decision drivers, workarounds, and where design system guidance was or wasn't being applied.

Focus areas AI workflow decisions · Guidance discovery · Trust signaling approaches · Contribution barriers
Method Workflow and decision-driver mapping alongside interviews to surface real implementation patterns
03

Design System Usage Review

Behavioral patterns

Reviewed existing design system usage patterns to understand where guidance was being applied and where gaps were leading to duplicated effort across teams.

Output Identified discoverability gaps and contribution pathway friction that were limiting ecosystem reuse
04

Generative AI Landscape Review

Strategic context

Reviewed emerging generative AI interaction patterns across the industry to contextualize internal findings and identify where the platform's design system needed to evolve beyond conventional chat-based interaction models.

Key finding Teams were already exploring multimodal inputs, embedded prompts, and iterative generation flows — requiring more flexible and scalable interaction patterns
Research Impact

From Insight to Decision

Each finding mapped directly to a design system investment or strategic decision.

Findings

Key Insights

Three primary findings that directly shaped design system investment, and one supporting finding that deepened the strategic picture.

Primary findings — design system-driving

01 · Discoverability

Guidance Discoverability Drives Adoption

Teams were significantly more likely to apply design system guidance when it was embedded within their existing workflows rather than requiring a separate discovery step. Guidance that required separate discovery was less likely to be applied.

Decision influenced

Broader and more accessible publishing of AI guidance across the platform

02 · Trust Patterns

Clear Patterns Are Critical for Communicating AI Behavior

Teams needed shared guidance on signaling AI involvement, communicating processing states, connecting prompts to outputs, and reducing cognitive load. Without these patterns, each team solved the same trust problem independently — and inconsistently.

Decision influenced

System feedback component and AI interaction pattern library prioritized and launched

03 · Ecosystem

Contribution Barriers Limited Ecosystem Growth

Teams were willing to contribute reusable components back to the design system — but perceived complexity in the contribution process reduced participation. The result was duplicated effort across teams building the same solutions in isolation.

Decision influenced

Contribution pathway improvements reducing duplication and improving system scalability

Additional finding — deepened the strategic picture

04 · Interaction Models

AI Workflows Extend Beyond Chat

Builder teams were already exploring multimodal inputs, embedded prompts, and iterative generation flows — requiring more flexible and scalable interaction patterns beyond conventional chat-based models.

Researcher Reflection
"Design systems succeed not only through patterns and documentation, but through clarity, trust, and shared ownership across the teams they serve."

This work demonstrated how foundational research can guide design systems through emerging technological shifts — not just by surfacing what teams need, but by understanding why existing guidance wasn't reaching them.

By understanding builder workflows and contribution barriers, the research shaped both AI design guidance and how teams collaborated across the platform — reinforcing that ecosystem health is as much a research problem as a design one.
